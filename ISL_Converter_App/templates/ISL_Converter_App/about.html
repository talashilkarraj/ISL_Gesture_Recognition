A sign is a form of nonverbal communication that uses body parts, hand shapes, hand positions and movements, arms, facial expressions, or lip movements instead of oral communication. Dynamic gestures include strokes, prestrokes, postures, and phases, whereas configurations are static gestures. The most important aspect of Indian Sign Language (ISL) is that it does not include grammar. Sign language, like spoken languages and dialects, has been designed differently depending on region and culture.
This system will undoubtedly benefit society by assisting physically disabled Indians. The majority of people struggle to understand ISL gestures. This has resulted in a communication schism between those who understand ISL and those who do not. It is not always possible to find an interpreter to translate these gestures when they are required.
A potential solution was implemented to facilitate this communication, which would translate hand poses and gestures from ISL in real-time. This system will serve as an aid for a deaf-mute to communicate with ordinary people via computer.
