Bridging the Gap

One of the society's fundamental flaws is the social divide that exists between people who are disabled or handicapped and people who have all of their human faculties in working conditions. Communication, which is the foundation of human progress, is frequently an impediment for those who are unable to communicate their thoughts. According to a World Health Organization report, 63 million individuals in India suffer from complete or partial deafness, with at least 50 lakhs of them being children. Hearing disorders account for 8.36 percent of all disabilities in India, according to the JICA statistical profile on disability in India. Speech disorders account for 5.06 percent. The lack of appropriate tools to overcome this communication barrier has resulted in societal inequity.

    This website is our attempt to tackle the problem, and create a feasible and easy-to-use solution.
